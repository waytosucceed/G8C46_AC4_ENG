Example 1
Prompt:

‚ÄúShould schools implement a year-round education system instead of the traditional academic calendar?‚Äù

Excerpt Example:

Claim:

Schools should implement a year-round education system because it promotes better retention of knowledge among students. Research has shown that students who attend year-round school perform better on standardised tests compared to those on a traditional schedule. The frequent breaks throughout the year allow students to rejuvenate without losing the knowledge they gained, leading to a more consistent learning experience.

Counterclaim:
Opponents argue that a year-round education system can disrupt family vacations and summer activities, which traditionally occur during the long summer break. They believe that students need extended time off to unwind and recharge.

Conclusion:
Despite these concerns, the advantages of a year-round education system, such as improved academic performance and increased retention of information, outweigh the drawbacks. By implementing this system, schools can create a more effective and engaging learning environment for all students, preparing them better for future challenges.

Example 2

Prompt
‚ÄúShould schools implement stricter regulations on the use of smartphones during class?‚Äù

Claim:
Stricter regulations on smartphone use in class should be implemented because it minimises distractions and enhances focus. Studies have shown that students who are restricted from using smartphones during lessons demonstrate better engagement and higher academic achievement. Limiting phone use also fosters stronger interpersonal interactions, encouraging students to collaborate and communicate effectively with peers.

Counterclaim:
Critics argue that smartphones are valuable learning tools, providing instant access to educational apps, research materials, and collaborative platforms. Banning them entirely might hinder students from utilising these resources to enhance their understanding of complex subjects.

Conclusion:
While smartphones can be beneficial in certain contexts, the negative impact of their misuse on concentration and productivity supports the need for stricter regulations. By implementing clear policies, schools can strike a balance between leveraging technology for learning and maintaining an environment conducive to academic success.
